This structure is actually very well suited to VR. A one-shot scene (no camera cuts) is a natural fit for XR because the player occupies the space like a theatre audience. Think of it as a hybrid of immersive theatre, walking simulator, and interactive drama.

Working Title

The Last Audience
A One-Shot XR Interactive Theatre Experience

"In the court of Melaka, loyalty and justice become indistinguishable."

High Concept

The player is an unseen court attendant inside the Balairong Seri (royal audience hall) during the final confrontation between Hang Tuah and Hang Jebat.

The player never becomes either hero.

Instead, they witness history while subtly influencing the emotional rhythm of the scene.

Runtime

10–15 minutes

No loading.

No scene transitions.

Everything occurs inside one palace hall.

Interaction Philosophy

The player never changes history.

Instead, they become part of the performance.

Their role is to:

witness
illuminate
accompany
amplify

This mirrors the role of a theatre stage crew or a wayang kulit audience, where participation shapes the experience without rewriting the story.

Three.js XR Technical Scope

This concept is intentionally achievable as a compact vertical slice using modern WebXR.

Environment

Single palace interior with baked global illumination and dynamic torch lights.
One continuous scene graph (no loading transitions).

Characters

Two animated skeletal characters (Hang Tuah and Hang Jebat).
Background NPCs (guards and ministers) with idle animation loops.

Interaction

XR controller raycasting for examining objects.
Trigger volumes to advance dialogue.
Rhythm interaction synchronized with sword impacts.
Torch-light interaction using grab or touch mechanics.
Spatial audio for dialogue, rain, thunder, and percussion.

Everything unfolds in a single uninterrupted take, emphasizing presence and theatricality. Rather than treating the Hang Tuah–Hang Jebat duel as an action game, this design frames it as a living stage performance where the player acts as the unseen witness whose attention, rhythm, and interaction shape the emotional experience without altering the historical outcome.
